Do I need to do anything to get the new NC minimum limits?

If your policy renewed after July 1, 2025, your insurance company should have automatically updated your coverage to meet the new 50/100/50 minimums. Check your declarations page to confirm. If you still see 30/60/25, contact your agent or insurance company immediately.

How much more will insurance cost with the new minimums?

According to the NC Department of Insurance, the increase is expected to be relatively smallâ€”typically only a few dollars per month for most drivers upgrading from old to new minimums. If you're seeing a large increase, it may be time to shop around. As an independent agent, I can compare multiple carriers to find you better rates.

Will my insurance cover me if I have an accident on New Year's Eve?

Yes, your auto insurance provides the same coverage on holidays as any other day. However, if you're found to be driving under the influence, your claim could be denied and your policy could be cancelled. More importantly, NC DWI convictions stay on your driving record and can increase your insurance rates for years.

What's changed about UM/UIM coverage in North Carolina?

The new law eliminates the "liability setoff" rule. Previously, if you were hit by an underinsured driver, your UIM insurance could deduct what their insurance paid. Now, you can collect BOTH the at-fault driver's full liability limits AND your full UIM coverage. This is a huge improvement for accident victims.

My teen just got their license. How does that affect insurance in 2026?

For drivers licensed after July 1, 2025, the "inexperienced operator surcharge" period extends from 3 years to 8 years. The surcharges decrease over time, but this makes it even more important to shop for the best rates and look for good student discounts. I can help you find carriers with the best teen driver rates.
