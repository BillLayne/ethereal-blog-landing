1

Red Cars Cost More to Insure

FALSE ‚ùå

This is perhaps the most enduring insurance myth in America! Insurance companies don't even ask about your car's color on applications. What actually affects your premium:

  • Make, model, and year
  • Engine size and horsepower
  • Safety features and crash test ratings
  • Theft rates for that specific vehicle
2

Your Credit Score Affects NC Insurance Rates

TRUE ‚úì

Many NC drivers are surprised to learn this is TRUE. In North Carolina, insurers can and do use credit-based insurance scores. Studies show drivers with poor credit can pay up to 67% more than those with excellent credit for the same coverage!

Pro Tip: Improving your credit score can lower your insurance rates. Pay bills on time and reduce credit card balances to see savings at renewal time.

3

North Carolina's minimum liability of 30/60/25 might satisfy legal requirements, but it leaves you dangerously exposed. Consider: the average cost of a serious injury claim exceeds $80,000, yet minimum coverage only provides $30,000 per person.

What Every NC Driver Should Know

NC Rate Bureau System

North Carolina is one of few states with a regulated rate system. All insurers must file rates with the NC Rate Bureau, which helps prevent extreme price variations.

Safe Driver Incentive Plan (SDIP)

NC uses a unique point system. Violations add points that increase your rates, while a clean record earns you discounts. Points stay on your record for 3 years.

At-Fault State

NC follows "pure contributory negligence" ‚Äî if you're even 1% at fault in an accident, you may be barred from recovering damages from other parties.

July 2025 Law Changes

New NC laws require higher UM/UIM coverage minimums. Make sure your policy is updated to comply with the latest requirements!
